About Us

Master Charles Hwang

 

Master Charles Hwang was born in Korea, and began his Martial Arts training at the age of nine. His training began with Taekwondo, then he moved to Kickboxing, and later to Hapkido. Master Hwang was lucky to learn Hapkido from Young Sul Choi, who is known as a father of this style.
In 1982 he held the title of World Heavy Weight Kickboxing Champion at the World Amateur Kickboxing Tournament.


In 1997 he started teaching the SRT - Special Reaction Team - of the 728th Military Police Battalion.


In 2005, Master Hwang got a gold medal on a World Cup Martial Art Competition held in Milano, Italy.


As of now, he has an 8th degree black belt in Hapkido and a 7th degree black belt in Kickboxing (Kwon Kyuk Do Style). He also has a 7th degree black belt in Tae Kwon Do and a 6th degree black belt in Kendo. Grand Master Hwang was honored with a 7th degree black belt in Shaolin Kung Fu and Tai Chi. He is also a Hwarang Sword Master as well as certified Sports Massage Therapist.
